# Our Shared Commitments

This class contract was shaped by what you told us in the Week 1 survey. It reflects your values, preferences, and goals for this tutorial. By agreeing to this contract, we commit to creating a learning environment where everyone can thrive.

---

## 1. Respect, Safety, and Inclusion

- We treat every person in this room with **respect and dignity**, regardless of background, identity, or viewpoint.
- We create a **safe space** where people can express their opinions freely, even when those opinions differ from our own. Disagreement is welcome; disrespect is not.
- We engage with ideas critically and charitably — attacking arguments, not people.
- Many of us bring **personal connections** to the topics we discuss — including lived experiences of conflict, migration, and inequality. We handle these topics with care.
- We practise **steelmanning**: constructing the strongest version of an opposing argument before critiquing it.

## 2. Participation

- We recognise that **not everyone is equally comfortable speaking up**. Several of you told us you find it hard to speak in groups or feel nervous about contributing aloud. That's completely fine.
- Participation takes many forms: speaking, active listening, written contributions, note-taking, and contributing to group deliverables all count.
- Where possible, we will provide **alternative ways to contribute** (e.g. written responses, Padlet posts) so that quieter voices can still be heard.
- If group work feels challenging, let your tutor know. We will find ways to make it work for you.

## 3. Preparation and Engagement

- We come to tutorials having completed the week's readings and quiz. This is essential for meaningful discussion.
- We engage seriously with the material, even when it challenges our existing views.
- We ask questions when something is unclear — there are no bad questions.
- Some of you mentioned finding the readings difficult. We will **work through key passages together** and highlight the main takeaways to support comprehension.

## 4. Building Skills Together

- You told us you want to develop **critical thinking and analytical skills**, improve your **essay writing and argument structure**, and learn to **apply theory to real-world issues**. The tutorial activities are designed to build exactly these skills.
- We will scaffold readings and provide guidance. Visual aids — concept maps, diagrams, structured comparisons — will be a regular feature, as many of you told us you are visual learners.
- You value **small-group discussions**, **connecting theory to evidence**, and **close reading exercises** — these will be central to our approach.
- We will support your reading comprehension skills throughout the semester, building confidence over time.

## 5. Real-World Connection

- You are excited about examining issues like the **Middle East conflict**, **armed conflict and war**, **US politics and Trumpism**, **gender and equality**, **the rise of the far right**, and **Australian domestic politics** through a political-theory lens. We will connect theory to these contemporary issues wherever possible.
- We recognise that these are not abstract topics for everyone in the room — some of us have **direct personal and family connections** to these events. We will engage with them seriously and respectfully.
- Healthy debate and discussion are encouraged. Bouncing ideas off each other is how we learn.

## 6. Practical Matters

- **Breaks**: You don't need to ask permission to use the bathroom. Step out quietly and return when you're ready.
- **Devices**: Laptops and tablets are welcome for note-taking and class activities. Please keep phones on silent.
- **Accessibility**: If you have specific learning needs or accommodations (e.g. through Monash DSS), please let your tutor know as early as possible so we can support you.
- **Communication**: If you have concerns about the course, your learning, or anything else — talk to your tutor or email [charles.crabtree@monash.edu](mailto:charles.crabtree@monash.edu). Early communication is always better.

---

## Our Agreement

By participating in this tutorial, we agree to uphold these commitments. This is a living document — if something isn't working, raise it and we will revisit it together.
