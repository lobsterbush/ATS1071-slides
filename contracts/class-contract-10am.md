# Our Shared Commitments

This class contract was shaped by what you told us in the Week 1 survey. It reflects your values, preferences, and goals for this tutorial. By agreeing to this contract, we commit to creating a learning environment where everyone can thrive.

---

## 1. Respect and Inclusion

- We treat every person in this room with dignity, regardless of background, identity, or viewpoint.
- We engage with ideas critically and charitably — attacking arguments, not people.
- We recognise that many of us bring personal connections to the topics we discuss (e.g. conflicts in the Middle East, US politics, the rise of the far right). We handle these topics with care and sensitivity.
- We practise **steelmanning**: constructing the strongest version of an opposing argument before critiquing it.

## 2. Participation

- Everyone's voice matters. We follow the principle: **everyone speaks once before anyone speaks twice**.
- We recognise that not everyone is equally comfortable speaking up in groups. That's okay — participation also includes active listening, note-taking, and contributing to group deliverables.
- If group work feels challenging, let your tutor know. We can find ways to make it work for you.
- You told us you value **small-group discussions**, **concept mapping**, and **connecting theory to real-world evidence** — we will prioritise these activities.

## 3. Preparation and Engagement

- We come to tutorials having completed the week's readings and quiz. This is essential for meaningful discussion.
- We engage seriously with the material, even when it challenges our existing views.
- We ask questions when something is unclear — there are no bad questions.
- Many of you are first-year students encountering political theory for the first time. That's the whole point — we will build skills together from the ground up.

## 4. Building Skills Together

- You told us you want to develop **critical thinking and analytical skills**, improve your **essay writing**, and learn to **apply theory to real-world issues**. The tutorial activities are designed to build exactly these skills week by week.
- We will scaffold readings and provide guidance. If a text is difficult, we will work through it together.
- Visual aids (concept maps, diagrams, argument structures) will be a regular feature of our tutorials.

## 5. Real-World Connection

- You are excited about examining issues like the **Middle East conflict**, **US politics and Trumpism**, **global armed conflict**, **the far right**, and **economic inequality** through a political-theory lens. We will connect theory to these contemporary issues wherever possible.
- We recognise that these are not abstract topics for everyone in the room — some of us have direct personal connections to these events.

## 6. Practical Matters

- **Breaks**: You don't need to ask permission to use the bathroom. Step out quietly and return when you're ready.
- **Devices**: Laptops and tablets are welcome for note-taking and class activities. Please keep phones on silent.
- **Communication**: If you have concerns about the course, your learning, or anything else — talk to your tutor or email [charles.crabtree@monash.edu](mailto:charles.crabtree@monash.edu). Early communication is always better.

---

## Our Agreement

By participating in this tutorial, we agree to uphold these commitments. This is a living document — if something isn't working, raise it and we will revisit it together.
