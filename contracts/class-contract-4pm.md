# Our Shared Commitments

This class contract was shaped by what you told us in the Week 1 survey. It reflects your values, preferences, and goals for this tutorial. By agreeing to this contract, we commit to creating a learning environment where everyone can thrive — even in an online setting.

---

## 1. Respect and Inclusion

- We treat every person in this class with dignity, regardless of background, identity, or viewpoint.
- We engage with ideas critically and charitably — attacking arguments, not people.
- We recognise that many of us bring personal connections to the topics we discuss — including lived experiences of conflict, migration, and political upheaval in places like the **Middle East**, **South Asia**, and **Central Asia**. We handle these topics with care and sensitivity.
- We practise **steelmanning**: constructing the strongest version of an opposing argument before critiquing it.

## 2. Participation in an Online Environment

- We acknowledge that **learning online comes with unique challenges**. Several of you told us you would have preferred in-person classes and that the online format makes engagement harder. We hear you, and we will work to make this tutorial as interactive and engaging as possible.
- **Cameras on** is encouraged when you're comfortable — it makes discussion feel more connected — but it is not required.
- Participation takes many forms: speaking, using the chat, responding to polls, contributing to shared documents, and engaging in breakout room activities all count.
- We will use **breakout rooms for small-group work** to create a more intimate discussion environment and to help build connections with your peers.
- If you have suggestions for making the online format work better, please share them. We want to improve this together.

## 3. Preparation and Engagement

- We come to tutorials having completed the week's readings and quiz. This is essential for meaningful discussion.
- We engage seriously with the material, even when it challenges our existing views.
- We ask questions when something is unclear — there are no bad questions. Use the chat or unmute.
- Some of you mentioned wanting to learn how to **properly read and interpret the readings**. We will work through key passages together and highlight the main takeaways.

## 4. Building Skills Together

- You told us you want to develop **critical thinking and analytical skills**, become better at **deconstructing arguments**, improve your **essay writing**, and learn to **apply theory to real-world issues**. The tutorial activities are designed to build exactly these skills week by week.
- Several of you expressed concern about **writing confidence**. We will build writing skills progressively and provide feedback opportunities throughout the semester.
- You value **concept mapping**, **connecting theory to evidence**, **structured comparison of theorists**, and **close reading exercises** — these will be central to our approach.
- We will scaffold readings and provide guidance. If a text is difficult, we will work through it together.

## 5. Real-World Connection

- You are excited about examining issues like the **Middle East conflict**, **US politics and Trumpism**, **the rise of the far right and populism**, **economic inequality and trade**, **armed conflict**, and **religion and politics** through a political-theory lens. We will connect theory to these contemporary issues wherever possible.
- You want to be able to **apply what you learn to current events** — reading news articles with a sharper analytical eye, engaging in more sophisticated political discussions, and understanding the world more deeply. That is exactly what this course is designed to do.
- We recognise that these are not abstract topics for everyone — some of us have **direct personal and family connections** to these events. We will engage with them seriously and respectfully.

## 6. Building Community Online

- Being a **first-year student in an online class** can feel isolating. We will actively work to build a sense of community — through breakout room discussions, collaborative activities, and shared digital spaces.
- If you prefer smaller group interactions, let your tutor know. We can adjust group sizes in breakout rooms.
- We encourage you to introduce yourselves to your breakout room partners and to engage beyond the formal activities where you feel comfortable doing so.

## 7. Practical Matters

- **Devices**: Please keep your microphone muted when not speaking to reduce background noise. Use the chat for quick responses or questions.
- **Accessibility**: If you have specific learning needs or accommodations (e.g. through Monash DSS), please let your tutor know as early as possible so we can support you.
- **Communication**: If you have concerns about the course, your learning, or anything else — talk to your tutor or email [charles.crabtree@monash.edu](mailto:charles.crabtree@monash.edu). Early communication is always better.

---

## Our Agreement

By participating in this tutorial, we agree to uphold these commitments. This is a living document — if something isn't working, raise it and we will revisit it together.
